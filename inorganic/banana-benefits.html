Why should we eat Banana?

Posted on 23rd Sep 2018 at 9:00 PM


Bananas are one of the most widely consumed edible fruits in the world and packed with nutrients. Banana plants are belongs to Plantae kingdom. The banana plant is the largest herbaceous flowering plant in the genus Musa. Musa is a family of Musaceae and order of Zingiberales. They were originally found in Southeast Asia and South Pacific and are grown in many warmer areas of the world.

Bananas are healthy source and contains high amounts of vitamine B6, vitamin C, Potassium, Magnesium and Maganese. It also contains Dietary Fiber and proteins. There are many different types, and they come in many different sizes and color. The color may be green, yellow, red, purple, or brown when ripe.



NUTRITION FACTS ABOUT BANANAS

  • Bananas are rich in minerals called Potassium.
  • It contain good levels of protein and Dietary Fiber.
  • They may help prevent asthma, cancer, high blood pressure, diabetes, cardiovascular disease, and digestive problems.
  • Bananas contain other beneficial plant compounds and antioxidants as well, including dopamine and catechin
  • The most common types of sugar found in ripe bananas are sucrose, fructose and glucose. In ripe bananas, the starch in it turns into sugar and unripe bananas contain carbohydrate (starch).


HEALTHY BENEFITS FOR BANANAS

Like most natural foods, bananas may have a number of health benefits


HEART HEALTHY

Bananas are good for your heart. The fiber, potassium, vitamin C and B6 content in bananas all support heart health. They are packed with mineral electrolyte that keeps electricity flowing throughout your body, which is required to keep your heart beating. Bananas' high potassium and low sodium content may also help protect your cardiovascular system against high blood pressure, according to the FDA.


BLOOD PRESSURE

Low in Sodium intake, helping to maintain normal blood pressure and sometimes lowering elevated blood pressure however increase Potassium intake which helps to maintain fluid levels in the human body and regulates the movements of nutrients and waste products in and out of cells.


DIGESTION AND WEIGHT LOSS

Bananas are particularly high in resistant starch, a form of dietary in fiber, which can help keep you regular and healthy and control your blood sugar. One banana can provide nearly 10 percent of your daily fiber requirement. Vitamin B6 can also help protect against type 2 diabetes and aid in weight loss. But if you do excersise and having bananas as breakfast or in morning hours with other fruits can helps in burning down the stored fat contain. In general, bananas are a great weight loss food because they taste sweet and are filling, which helps curb cravings.Unripe, green bananas contain considerable amounts of resistant starch and pectin, which are types of dietary fiber.Resistant starch and pectins act as prebiotic nutrients, which support the growth of beneficial gut bacteria.


DEPRESSION AND MOOD

Bananas can be helpful to reduce or overcoming in depression. Bananas contain tryptophan, an amino acid that boosting your mood and helps you to sleep well and relax your muscles.


SUMMARY

  • Bananas are very healthy and nutritious fruit.
  • Eating upto 2 to 3 bananas in a day maximum. If you eat more bananas it aids in weight gain and high sugar content. So consume it moderately.
  • However eating bananas in excess may trigger headaches and sleeping.
  • It is very much essential for protecting your eyes, maintaining normal vision and improving vision at night
  • Bananas may not be overflowing with calcium, but they are still helpful in keeping bones strong.
  • It improved your heart and digestive health when consumed regularly as a part of a healthy lifestyle.
  • Although bananas are unsuitable on a low-carb diet and may cause problems for some diabetics, overall they are an incredibly to eating daily.
